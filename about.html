About

I'm Brian Utesch.

I have spent my career as a UX researcher, much of it running research and watching good findings die on the way to a decision.

That gap is why these tools exist. A team can run a flawless study and still lose, because the recommendation never gets adopted and nobody can see where it broke. I co-authored the Recommendation Adoption Score with Tammi Fitzwater and published it through Nielsen Norman Group to put a number on that problem. I co-authored UMUX-Lite years earlier for the same reason, because a measure you can defend beats an opinion you cannot.

Every tool in Polite Robot follows one rule. A deterministic engine computes every number, and the AI writes only the words around it. Nothing scores a study on a feeling, so you can audit the math, and when it is wrong you can point to exactly where.

I build these myself, in Wake Forest, North Carolina. If a tool tells you something, I want you to be able to check it.
